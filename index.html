Crabtree · Movement Mechanics

The Nonviolence Premium

A two-paper series on whether nonviolent protest moves policy more than violent protest does

Two companion papers, by Charles Crabtree and John B. Holbein, ask the same question at two scales — U.S. counties (2017–2023) and country-years (1990–2020) — using the same estimand: a between-arm contrast Δ = ATTnv − ATTv that differences two separately-identified within-unit ATTs (each arm against never-treated controls, with unit and year fixed effects). The design does not exploit within-unit variation between the nonviolent and violent arms, because counties and countries rarely switch tactical character across the panel. The combined finding: once the Nonviolence Premium is estimated this way at the county or country scale, rather than as a between-campaign comparison of the kind Chenoweth and Stephan (2011) pioneered, it does not survive at either scale.

Two papers, one estimand

Paper 1 · U.S. county-year

The Nonviolence Premium: Local Policy Response in the United States, 2017–2023

47,859 county-years from the Crowd Counting Consortium (CCC), mapped to Census of Governments per-capita public-safety spending. Headline arm contrast = −$10,588 per capita (SE $7,843; p = 0.177) — backlash-signed but statistically indistinguishable from zero, and identified by a 2020 cohort whose budget data is contaminated by American Rescue Plan public-safety transfers.

Paper 2 · Cross-national

The Nonviolence Premium: Evidence from 166 Countries, 1990–2020

Mass Mobilization v5.1 protest events joined to V-Dem polyarchy, Polity V regime indicators, and World Bank WDI fiscal outcomes. Four difference-in-differences estimators — Callaway–Sant'Anna, synthetic DiD, augmented synthetic control, and PanelMatch — all return statistically zero contrasts. The Chenoweth–Stephan 3.5% cliff rests on only 4 violent-maximalist country-years against 30 nonviolent ones.

Two-paper headline. Once the Nonviolence Premium is estimated as a fixed-effects arm contrast at the county or country scale — not as a between-campaign comparison of the Chenoweth–Stephan kind — it does not survive at either scale. The Intensity-Equivalence null (no difference between nonviolent and violent arms) is the most parsimonious reading across both papers. Every headline number is reproducible end-to-end from data/raw/ via the numbered scripts in R/.

Replication

The full pipeline lives in github.com/lobsterbush/movement-mechanics. The numbered R/A_US_*.R and R/A_MM_*.R scripts run the analysis end-to-end, emitting per-step macros that the paper/A_nonviolence_premium_*/paper.tex files \input. Raw data is excluded from the repo and pulled from Harvard Dataverse via R/00_download_*.R.
