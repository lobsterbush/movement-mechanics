The Question

Does the nonviolence premium travel?

Chenoweth & Stephan's 3.5%-of-population claim is built on campaign-type comparisons. We re-estimate it as a within-country treatment effect.

If nonviolent discipline is the tactical variable that matters, we should see it in V-Dem polyarchy whether we ask the question in U.S. counties or in country-years.

Reading A_US and A_MM Together

The premium does not survive at either scale.

U.S. counties (A_US)

Backlash-signed but insignificant; identified by a 2020 cohort whose CoG fiscal data is contaminated by ARPA.

Cross-national (A_MM)

Clean null across four estimators. No single-year fiscal-shock confounder.

Combined reading: Intensity Equivalence is the conclusion that survives once the 2020 U.S. confounder is removed.

Bottom Line

Once nonviolence is a within-unit treatment, the cross-national premium is statistically zero.

The Chenoweth–Stephan 3.5%-cliff is consistent with our data only because the violent maximalist arm is empirically rare. A within-country test cannot reject the null.
